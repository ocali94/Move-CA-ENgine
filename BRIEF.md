# Move CA Engine — Team Brief

**What it is:** an internal web app that runs the front end of Move's client-acquisition funnel in one place. It helps you **qualify a lead, prep the call, build the proposal, and read the market** — faster, and in Move's own voice and format.

Think of it as a smart assistant that has read all of Move's playbooks and real sent proposals, and does the first draft of the busywork so you can focus on the client.

---

## The four tools

### 1. Lead Qualifier
Paste a brand name, a website link, or your notes about a prospect. You get back:
- A **fit score out of 100** against Move's ideal client profile, with the reasons behind it.
- A **CRM-ready summary** you can copy straight into your notes.
- A recommended next step.

*Use it to decide, in seconds, whether a lead is worth your time — before you invest in it.*

### 2. Call Prep Engine
Paste the booking-form answers or intake notes. You get a **one-page battle card**:
- Brand snapshot and likely pain map
- 10 diagnostic questions to ask on the call
- The service path that probably fits
- What to verify, what to avoid, and a suggested opening angle

*Use it to walk into any discovery call fully prepped in a couple of minutes.*

### 3. Proposal Studio (the flagship)
Paste your discovery notes. The app pulls out the key facts (you can edit them), then builds the proposal **one section at a time**, in Move's real proposal format:
Cover & Context → Package Summary → Timeline → Recommendation → SLA → Scope of Work → Investment & Next Steps.

- **You approve each section** before it moves on — nothing is finalized without you.
- Every section is **checked against Move's proposal rules** (correct talent titles, proper tables, consistent pricing, no off-brand phrases) and flags anything to fix.
- It also drafts the **companion email**, and exports the finished proposal to a clean document.

*Use it to go from messy call notes to a polished, on-brand proposal in a fraction of the usual time.*

### 4. Market Signals
A live read on the demand environment Move's DTC clients are selling into:
- A **Demand Pulse** score (0–100) with a plain-English read on whether conditions are heating up or cooling down.
- Key indicators (consumer sentiment, disposable income, energy costs, and more) — each explained in one sentence, no economics degree required.
- A **Campaign Signal** with **ready-to-post tweets** written from that week's actual numbers.

*Use it for timely, market-aware outreach and talking points.*

Plus supporting screens: **Activity Feed** (what's been done), **Saved Projects**, **Reference Library** (Move's playbooks), and **Setup**.

## A few things worth knowing

- **The AI is always labeled.** When a real AI model writes something you'll see a green **"Live AI"** tag; if it ever falls back to the built-in logic, you'll see an amber **"Fallback"** tag. You'll never mistake one for the other. The header also shows which AI is running and lets you switch it — it's set up with a primary and an automatic backup, so it keeps working even if one provider is down.
- **You're always in control.** The app drafts; you decide. Nothing gets sent anywhere automatically, and proposals require your section-by-section approval.
- **It doesn't make up numbers.** Market figures come from public sources (the Fed's FRED data, Yahoo Finance). Proposals are grounded in your discovery notes and Move's real playbooks — not invented.
- **"Cached" market data is not stale data.** It means the latest published public figures are being reused rather than re-downloaded every visit. Those indicators only update monthly or quarterly, so it's current. Hit **Refresh market data** any time to pull the very latest.
- **Your work saves in your browser (for now).** In this version, projects are stored on the browser/machine you're using — they aren't shared across devices or teammates yet. Use **Export JSON** to back up or move your work. (Shared, multi-user storage is on the roadmap.)

## How to use it

1. Open the app and enter the **shared team access code** (ask Omar).
2. Pick a tool from the left sidebar.
3. Paste your input, click generate, review and tweak, then copy or export.

That's it — no setup, no training data to load. It already knows Move's playbooks.
